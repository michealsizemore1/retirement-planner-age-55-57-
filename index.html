🔁 Atomic Habits Tracker

Based on James Clear's Atomic Habits — Small habits, remarkable results
👤 IDENTITY — Who am I becoming?
💡 Focus on identity, not outcomes. "I am a runner" not "I want to run." Every action is a vote for the person you want to become.
👁 LAW 1 · Make It Obvious
"You don't rise to the level of your goals; you fall to the level of your systems."
e.g. "When I pour my morning coffee, I will put on my running shoes at the front door."
e.g. Bible left on the pillow, running shoes by the bed, fruit bowl on the counter.
e.g. "After I sit down with coffee, I will read my Bible for 10 minutes."
✨ LAW 2 · Make It Attractive
"The more attractive an opportunity is, the more likely it is to become habit-forming."
e.g. Only listen to your favourite podcast while running. Only watch Netflix while folding laundry.
e.g. Put on your running playlist before every workout — your brain learns the cue = time to perform.
e.g. Running group, Bible study, mastermind circle — join people for whom the habit is normal.
⚡ LAW 3 · Make It Easy
"Motion vs. action — planning feels productive but only action produces results."
e.g. "Read one page." "Do one push-up." "Lace up your shoes." Master the habit of showing up first.
e.g. Lay out workout clothes the night before. Pre-load the water bottle. Keep the book on the nightstand open.
e.g. Delete social media apps (reinstall to use). Unplug the TV after watching. Put junk food on the highest shelf.
🏆 LAW 4 · Make It Satisfying
"What is immediately rewarded is repeated. What is immediately punished is avoided."
e.g. Mark an X on a calendar, move a paper clip jar, check this box — visual progress is satisfying.
e.g. Missed your run yesterday? Non-negotiable: get out today even if just 10 min. Protect the streak identity.
e.g. Say "yes!" after finishing. Enjoy your favourite tea after reading. Let the feeling of success be the reward.
